Meadow's Whisper

C h a t G P T

In a meadow wide and free,
Underneath the old oak tree,
Lies a secret, soft and fair,
Whispers floating in the air.

Dewdrops lace the morning light,
Dancing diamonds, pure and bright.
Grass blades green, with edges gold,
Guarding tales, untold, old.

Sunrise paints the sky in hues,
Crimson, lavender, and blues.
Birds in chorus sing and soar,
Telling sagas of before.

In this field, where poppies bloom,
There's a silence, not of gloom,
But of peace, as if the land,
Holds the world in gentle hand.

Breezes hum a lullaby,
Clouds adrift in azure sky.
Nature's heart, in rhythm, beats,
In every flower, every leaf.

Here, beneath the vast expanse,
Given to a second glance,
Lies the poem of the earth,
Singing of rebirth, mirth.

So, when heavy weighs your soul,
Let this meadow make you whole.
For in nature’s embrace, tight,
You’ll find solace, love, and light.
